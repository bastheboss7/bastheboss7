## üóìÔ∏è 2. The Appraisal Lifecycle
I manage performance as a **continuous loop**, not a once-a-year surprise.

* **Q1 (Set):** Define 3-5 SMART goals using the [Skills Matrix](./SKILLS_MATRIX.md) to identify "Stretch" areas.
* **Q2 (Pivot):** Mid-year review. Adjust goals if project priorities have shifted (e.g., shifting focus from Selenium to Playwright).
* **Q3 (Push):** Focus on the "Leadership" pillar‚Äîspeaking at sessions or documenting complex architectures.
* **Q4 (Assess):** Final appraisal based on evidence collected in the [People Metrics](./PEOPLE_METRICS.md) dashboard.

## ‚öñÔ∏è 4. Handling Underperformance (The PIP Protocol)
If a team member consistently misses targets, I move to a **Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)** focused on support:
1. **Root Cause:** Is it a Skill gap, a Tooling gap, or a Motivation gap?
2. **Weekly Syncs:** Short-term, tactical goals with immediate feedback.
3. **Paired Support:** Pairing with a "High Performer" to bridge technical gaps.
